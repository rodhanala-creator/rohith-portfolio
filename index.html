01 Concept case study Healthcare ops

The dental practice that was drowning in its own admin.

A five-chair dental practice gave every channel one shared intake — and stopped losing urgent cases to sticky notes.

  • < 2 minFirst-response time, down from ~6 hours
  • 7 hrsAdmin time recovered per week
  • 1 inboxReplaced phone, email, walk-ins, social

The story

Patient enquiries arrived across phone, email, walk-ins, and social — each channel a separate pile of sticky notes. Urgent cases slipped past unanswered while the team ran flat out.

I shadowed the front desk for two days, mapped every patient touchpoint, then built one shared intake. Urgent cases now auto-route to an emergency queue, routine requests get a templated reply with a calendar link in seconds, and reminders go out automatically. The practice didn’t get more tools — it got fewer decisions to make.

  • Tally
  • Calendly
  • Gmail API
  • Airtable

02 Selected project Lead generation

A plumbing business with plenty of leads — and nowhere to put them.

Rebuilt a regional plumber’s lead funnel as a single calm pipeline — the system did the chasing so the team could stay on the tools.

  • 19% → 31%Website lead conversion
  • < 3 minFirst-response time, down from hours
  • +18Cold contacts re-engaged in 30 days

The story

Leads arrived from Google Ads, the website, Facebook, and word of mouth — but most never heard back inside the first hour. The owner wasn’t losing work to competitors. He was losing it to silence.

I audited the existing intake (three phone screenshots and a 1,400-email inbox), then built one landing page, one shared form, AI-drafted instant acknowledgement, and a New → Contacted → Qualified → Booked pipeline. Drip sequences nudge warm leads; a 30-day re-engagement flow wakes dormant ones.

  • Framer
  • Tally
  • Airtable
  • Make.com
  • Resend
  • OpenAI
  • Slack

03 Workflow redesign Web app

BookFlow — the booking and client app I built when no SaaS quite fit.

A focused web app for small service businesses — today’s schedule, live pipeline, client directory, and an AI panel that drafts the next message.

  • 1 screenReplaces calendar, sheet, CRM, messaging
  • 3 keysTo add and confirm a booking
  • 0 backendRuns in the browser, no lock-in

The story

Across two automation projects in the same month, small service teams kept stitching together a calendar, a spreadsheet, a CRM, and a messaging tool — none of which talked to each other.

I designed the workflow in Figma, built the working web app end-to-end in vanilla JavaScript, and shipped a public live demo with realistic seed data anyone can interact with. A focused tool designed around one workflow beats a bloated all-in-one platform every time.

  • Vanilla JS
  • CSS Grid
  • LocalStorage
  • OpenAI API
